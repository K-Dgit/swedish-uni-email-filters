How to set up your email filter

This takes about two minutes. You only need to do it once. Select your email platform below for step-by-step instructions.

1
Create a label
Open Gmail. In the left sidebar, scroll down and click "More", then "Create new label". Name it after your university (for example, "SU-Sweden"). Click Create.
2
Open the filter creator
In Gmail's search bar at the top, click the filter icon (the sliders icon on the right side of the search bar). This opens the advanced search form.
3
Paste your filter string
Go to the Filter Generator tab, select your university, and copy the filter string. Back in Gmail, paste it into the "From" field. Click "Create filter" at the bottom right.
4
Choose your filter actions
On the next screen, select these three options:
  • Star it
  • Apply the label (select the label from step 1)
  • Also apply filter to matching conversations
Click "Create filter".
5
Verify
Go to Gmail Settings (gear icon), then "See all settings", then the "Filters and Blocked Addresses" tab. Your filter should appear in the list.
1
Create a folder
In Outlook, right-click "Inbox" in the left sidebar and select "Create new subfolder" (desktop) or "New folder" (web). Name it after your university (for example, "SU-Sweden").
2
Open Rules settings
In Outlook on the web: click the gear icon (Settings), then "View all Outlook settings", then "Mail", then "Rules". Click "+ Add new rule". In Outlook desktop: go to File, then Manage Rules and Alerts, then New Rule.
3
Set the condition
Name the rule (for example, "Sweden university emails"). Under "Add a condition", select "From" and enter your university domain (for example, su.se). You will need to create separate rules for each domain: your university domain, si.se, migrationsverket.se, and ladok.se.
4
Set the actions
Under "Add an action", select:
  • Move to: select the folder from step 1
  • Flag message (to make it stand out)
Note: to keep a copy in your inbox as well, also add the action "Keep a copy in Inbox". Click "Save".
5
Repeat for each domain
Create one rule per domain. For example: one rule for @su.se, one for @si.se, one for @migrationsverket.se, and one for @ladok.se. All rules should move to the same folder and flag the message.
General steps for any email client

Most email clients (Apple Mail, Thunderbird, Yahoo Mail, ProtonMail, and others) support filtering or rules. The exact steps differ, but the logic is the same:

1
Create a folder or label
Create a dedicated folder or label for your Sweden-related emails. Name it something clear, like "SU-Sweden" or "Sweden Studies".
2
Find the filter or rules settings
Look for "Filters", "Rules", or "Message rules" in your email client's settings. This is usually found under Settings, Preferences, or Mail preferences.
3
Create a rule based on sender domain
Set the condition to: "From" contains your university domain (for example, su.se). Then set the action to move or label the email and mark it as important or starred.
4
Repeat for key domains
Create the same rule for each of the following domains: your university domain, si.se (Swedish Institute), migrationsverket.se (Migration Agency), and ladok.se (student admin system).
5
Test it
Send yourself a test email or wait for the next incoming message from one of these domains. Confirm it lands in the correct folder with the correct flag or star.

Adding new addresses later

Throughout your journey, you may receive study-related emails from addresses outside your university domain. For example, your embassy's migration section, or a form service. Here is how to add them.

1
Spot the new sender
When you receive a study-related email from an unfamiliar address, note the full sender address. For example, your embassy might email you from migration.abuja@gov.se, or a form service might send from no-reply@wufoo.com. These are outside your university domain, so the main filter will not catch them.
2
Verify it is legitimate
Before adding any address, check that it is real. Does the domain match a known institution? If you are unsure, verify with other admitted students on the Goin' app, your university's buddy network, or by contacting your university directly. See the "Staying Safe" tab for guidance on spotting fake emails.
3
Update your filter or rule
In Gmail: go to Settings, then "Filters and Blocked Addresses", find your filter, click "edit", then "continue", and add the new address with " OR " before it. In Outlook: create a new rule for the new address pointing to the same folder. In other clients: add the address to your existing rule or create a new one with the same action.
Build the habit: Every time a new Sweden-related email arrives, check if the sender is caught by your filter. If not, verify the address is legitimate and add it. This takes about 30 seconds and ensures you never miss a deadline buried in your inbox.

Staying safe with email

As an international student, you may be targeted by email scams impersonating your university, the Swedish Institute, the Migration Agency, or banks. Here is how to protect yourself.

How to verify an email sender
1
Check the actual email address, not the display name
Anyone can set any display name on an email. In Gmail, click the small arrow next to "to me" under the sender's name to see the real address. In Outlook, hover over or click the sender's name. If the address says something like admissions@stockholmuni-apply.com instead of study@su.se, it is not legitimate.
2
Verify the domain
Every Swedish university has one primary domain (for example, su.se for Stockholm University, kth.se for KTH). Official emails come from that domain or its subdomains (such as dsv.su.se). If the domain does not match your university, be suspicious. Swedish government agencies use .se domains: si.se, migrationsverket.se, ladok.se.
3
Never click payment links in emails
Your tuition fee is paid through Ladok at student.ladok.se. Always navigate there directly by typing the URL into your browser, never through a link in an email. The Swedish Migration Agency fee is paid at migrationsverket.se. No legitimate Swedish institution will ask you to pay fees through a link sent by email or to a private bank account.
4
When in doubt, verify independently
Do not reply to a suspicious email. Instead, go to universityadmissions.se, find your university's official contact email, and ask them directly whether the email was legitimate. You can also check with fellow students on the Goin' app or your university's buddy network.
Common scams targeting international students:

Fake housing offers: Someone claims to have an apartment and asks for a deposit before you can view it. Never pay for housing you have not verified in person or by video call.

Fake scholarship emails: An email claims you won a scholarship and requests personal details or bank information. The Swedish Institute publishes results only on si.se, never by unsolicited email.

Fake tuition requests: An email asks you to pay tuition to a different account. Tuition is only paid through student.ladok.se.

University impersonation: An email looks official but uses a slightly different domain (for example, su-university.se instead of su.se). Always check the actual sender address.
